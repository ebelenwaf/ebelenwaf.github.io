Ebelechukwu Nwafor

2300 Sixth Street, Room 2120B · Washington, DC 20059 · (555) 555-555 · ebelechukwu.nwafor(at)howard(dot)edu

I am an Assistant Professor at the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer science, Howard University. I completed my Ph.D in Computer Science from the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, Howard University under the supervision of Dr.Gedare Bloom. My research interests spans across cloud/mobile computing, data provenance, information visualization, health informatics and Internet of Things (IoT)/cyber-physical systems security. For my dissertation, I explored the applications of data provenance to the Internet of Things. A brief description of this project can be found here .

Research

Visual-based Anomaly Detection
Visual-based Anomaly Detection
With the vast proliferation of data on Internet-conncected devices, there exists a crucial need to develop detection mechanisms that can spot anomalous data propagation on a system.

This research looks at creating interactive visualization tools that detect anomalous data occurence on IoT devices.

Interactive Visualization and Prediction of Clinical Data Outcomes
Interactive Visualization and Prediction of Clinical Data Outcomes
Hospitals generate unprecedented amounts of data on a daily basis. From lab test results, to information related to a patients visit. This data, if properly harnesed can provide valuable insight to better manage and improve a patient disease outcome. Data visualization plays an important role in recognizing trends and patterns in data which could enable us predict the outcome of a clinical event.

This research looks at developing interactive visualization tools which allows clinicians and researchers a means to properly understand and predict disease outcomes.
